TEMPERATURE

In this visual, we see the effects that latitude has upon temperature in our sample cities.


  • In the Northern Hemisphere, maximum temperatures tend to vary more greatly. The higher in latitude, the lower the maximum temperature.

  • In the Southern Hemisphere, maximum temperatures vary far less, regardless of how far south one travels.

    • Although it may seem like maximum temperatures in the Southern Hemisphere are generally higher, this data could be affected by the location of the cities and their local climates, such as near oceans where temperatures are more moderate.


    They may also be located more closely together. We have no data related to the longitude of the sample cities.
