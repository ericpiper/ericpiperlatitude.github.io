HUMIDITY

In this visual, we see the effects that latitude has upon humidity in our sample cities.


  • High humidity is spread throughout the latitudes and is affects the majority of our sample cities.

  • High humidity is more concentrated toward the equator. Low humidy isn't strongly concentrated and occurs in few cities in mid-latitudes.

  • There is a bias of 20 degrees toward the Northern Hemisphere in this data set, with the northernmost cities reaching 80 degrees above the equator, while the southernmost cities reach only 60 degrees below the equator.

    • One might question why the lowest humidity levels wouldn't be nearest the poles instead of being more evenly spread. Cold air holds less moisture. But in this case, remember that we haven't any data related to the climates of these cities, which can have a profound impact upon the outcome.
